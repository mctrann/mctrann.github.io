Outreach Program

"Creative App Development and Design Youth Workshops" was one of Lincoln's first ever mobile application development workshops for kids founded by a fellow programmer and me in the Fall of 2016. Though Lincoln is on the rise in "Silicon Prairie", the youth have a lot to learn about the tech world. The STEM world has a stigma for being difficult and intimidating, thus we started CADDY to show kids they are capable of greatness as long as they put their mind to it. With the support from the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, CADDY helped about 30 or so kids in the Lincoln community learn the fundamentals of computational thinking through MIT's app inventor. Read more about our outreach here!

Internships

In the summer of 2017, I landed my dream internship at Spreetail. Finishing up my freshman year, I came onboard as a Software Engineer intern specifically working on the company's newly formed UI/UX department. I took charge of researching user experience within the company's software, conducting interviews with employees on their experience with specific software and prototyping various pages used by employees. I also assisted in developing visuals for company metrics seen throughout the campus and assisted in launching Spreetail's Internship page. Throughout this internship I gained skills in CSS/SASS, HTML, and JavaScript/Angular.

CSEA Education Chair

I was elected as the Education Chair for UNL's Computer Science and Engineering Ambassadors. My responsibilities include overseeing the Robotics After School Program throughout the middle schools of Lincoln and any other educational outreach program that are done throughout the community.

Girl's Scout Mentor

I was ignited with a passion for programming after my first mobile application camp sponsored by Girl's Scouts in the summer of 2015. A year later, I returned as a mentor and have continued my involvment with this camp to this day. The computing field can be intimidating especially for young women. This three day camp gave me the confidence to pursue my path in Computer Science. I hope to enstill that same confidence in the young women who participate in this camp. More information about this camp can be found here.

Experience in University

As I continue pursuing my degree in Computer Science at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, I have found a passion in helping others break down the barrier of intimidation they may find in the computing field. Within the Computer Science and Engineering department, I'm working as a teacher assistant for fundamental classes in the department like "Introduction to Computer Science" and "Computer Science I". These courses aim to introduce the basic concepts of computing to its students. My job is to help students understand these concepts. Currently, I plan to do research within the Human Robotic Interaction field with professors at the University throughout the 2018 year. My involvment with the CSCE department has helped further my skills and passion in the computing field.

Beyond Programming

When I'm not programming, my involvment goes back to my roots as a Vietnamese American. Inspired by those I've met throughout variuos conferences, from Social Chair to President, and with the assistance of my fellow council members, we've revived the Vietnamese Student Association on UNL's campus. What looked like a fading community, has been brought back up to an organization that's filled with members involved in a cultural show and a mentorship program. I've learned to keep my culture rooted in my life.
